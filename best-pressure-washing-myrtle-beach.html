Coastal Climate Challenges

  • Mold and Mildew Growth: Thrives in humid conditions, penetrating siding, stucco, and wood surfaces. Creates black, green, and gray staining while degrading materials and releasing spores that affect air quality.

  • UV Degradation: Intense South Carolina sun combined with salt exposure accelerates fading, chalking, and breakdown of exterior surfaces. Regular cleaning removes contaminants that worsen UV damage.

Best Practices for Myrtle Beach Property Maintenance

Regular pressure washing is essential for Myrtle Beach properties, but timing and frequency matter for optimal results and value.

Recommended Cleaning Schedule

  • Standard Myrtle Beach Homes: Annual house washing in spring or fall removes accumulated mold, mildew, and coastal buildup. Prevents permanent staining and material damage.
  • Oceanfront Properties: Bi-annual cleaning (spring and fall) necessary due to constant salt spray exposure accelerating corrosion and organic growth.
  • Driveways and Concrete: Annual cleaning or as needed when oil stains, tire marks, or organic growth appear. Sealing after cleaning extends protection.
  • Decks and Fences: Annual cleaning before applying new stain or sealant ensures proper adhesion and extends wood life. Spring cleaning prepares outdoor spaces for summer use.
  • Roofs: Clean every 2-3 years when black streaks (algae) become visible. Early treatment prevents extensive damage requiring premature replacement.
  • Commercial Properties: Quarterly to bi-annual cleaning depending on business type, foot traffic, and appearance standards.

Best Time of Year for Pressure Washing

Spring (March-May): Most popular season for pressure washing in Myrtle Beach. Removes winter buildup before summer heat, prepares properties for peak vacation season, and allows time for any discovered repairs before summer storms.

Fall (September-November): Second-best time for cleaning. Removes summer humidity buildup before winter, prevents mold and mildew from taking hold during cooler months, and prepares properties for holiday season. Often better availability and pricing than spring.

Avoid: Peak summer heat (July-August) when afternoon thunderstorms interrupt service daily. Mid-winter (December-February) when some cleaning solutions work less effectively in cold temperatures, though still acceptable for urgent needs.

What's the difference between pressure washing and soft washing?

Pressure washing and soft washing are two distinct methods used for different surfaces. Pressure washing uses high-pressure water (2,500-4,000 PSI) to physically blast away dirt, stains, and contaminants from durable surfaces like concrete driveways, sidewalks, brick, and stone. Soft washing uses low-pressure water (under 500 PSI) combined with specialized cleaning solutions to gently clean delicate surfaces like vinyl siding, painted wood, stucco, and roofs. The chemicals in soft washing do the cleaning work‚Äînot pressure‚Äîkilling mold, mildew, and algae at the root while protecting surfaces from damage. Professional companies use soft washing for house exteriors and pressure washing for concrete‚Äînever high pressure on siding, which causes gouging, forces water behind walls, strips paint, and creates expensive damage. When hiring, specifically ask which method they'll use for each surface. If they say "pressure washing" for your vinyl siding, find a different company.
